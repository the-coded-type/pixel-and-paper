## The Traveller

### Info

**Themes:** Night, stars, lights, glimmer.  
**Classification:** Gross, eerie.

**CW:** Aliens, conspiracies, tentacles, police, sickness, kidnapping, body infestation, body penetration, grief, loss of a close one.

**District:** Desert.

Brooke was originally written by Chris Airiau for the *Swine* UL (Urban Legends book) and has been adapted for *The Traveller**.***

#### Conditions

Roll 1d6:

1. A small burn scar draws a weird diagram on your skin.  
2. Thin, protruding, iridescent tentacles burst through your flesh.  
3. You feel mesmerized by bright lights and compelled to stare at them.  
4. Darkness troubles you in a profound way.  
5. You fixate on coincidences, perceiving them as irrefutable proof that you are being spied on.  
6. You have doubts about who you are, what you are.

### Legend

**What the players know.**

It began on a Wednesday, a warm, summer evening in the Bay. After the sun set, the sky was abruptly cleaved by a comet. Everything came to a halt: dogs quieted, music faded, even the summer breeze stilled as shuddering leaves froze mid-air. There was only the comet. You were there. You saw it. It felt sacred, like a birth.

When the light disappeared, the night shrouded the sky again, and the usual summer cacophony filled the air. 

That was a few days ago. Ever since, your restless nights are plagued by vivid dreams of a sphere of light, cold, metallic, hovering above your corpse.

Quickly, you realized you weren't alone in these visions. Whispers about the falling star and the dream figure began to spread, and, like everyone else, you started calling it the **Traveller**.

A bone-deep feeling tells you the **Traveller** will appear today at an old Desert crossroads. You’ve just arrived there, guided by instinct and the shared dreams of strangers met at gas stations along the way.

### Truth

**What’s really happening.**

The Faceless God has injected one of its offspring, the Traveller, into reality. The Traveller is a scout, a probe, with a hunger for learning what humans feel and what they fear. It infests their dreams and lures with the promise of a better life, devoid of pain and loneliness. When it gets close to lost souls, it contaminates them, injecting a **Nucleus** into their soft flesh belly. The Nucleus consumes their thoughts and emotions, grows, and ultimately rips their body apart.

Hints about the connection between the Traveller and the Faceless God are disseminated throughout the Urban Legend. Unveiling them is not required to successfully survive the UL; however, their discoveries can be a nice reward for players who approach the adventure as an investigation.  

The **Collectors** (p. XX) believe the Traveller is a god and are desperately trying to get ahold of a Nucleus. **Channel 7** (p. XX) exploits the Comet apparition to boost audience ratings by spreading false information, as usual.

### Before We Start

This Urban Legend holds clues scattered throughout each Beat. However, there isn’t one unique way to “solve” the mystery of the Traveller. The players don’t need to unravel all the threads to play the adventure and survive the hardships presented to them. Clues and hints are just hooks to facilitate running the adventure. The GM and the players are encouraged to fill the blanks left in between.


### BEAT 1: First Encounter

Desert, Crossroads, Night.

It's 2am, the night is cold, and you've been waiting all day at the expected landing point for the Traveller. A couple dozen cars and trucks form a circle. At the center, flowers, fruits, wishes written on folded pieces of paper, and other objects form a pile of welcome gifts.

Silver ribbons adorn a multi-colored sign that says, "We always believed." They flap in the wind like small, ecstatic hands.

**Iris**, an elderly woman, is talking to her scared little dog. The wind intermittently brings the mellow sound of a chant in an unknown language (see **Victoria**, p. XX).

Parked close by is a small camper van, a relic of the 70s. White and beige, it's tidy and clean, yet shows the wear of age, much like its owners. They're a pair of young retirees, **Claire** and **Alfred**.   
They’re warming a teapot on a gas camping stove. They’re eager to share their rose tea.

#### The Encounter

The wind picks up, swirling clouds of dust. The folded papers and other gifts are swept away, and the little dog barks frantically. A bright, intense light appears in the sky above. Some people begin chanting, while others become ecstatic: "They're coming back\!" A trucker runs to their vehicle and locks themself inside. As the sphere descends, the cars extinguish their lights.

The sphere halts, hovering twenty meters above the landing site: the Traveller, a brilliant orb of light. Its size is impossible to discern, unlike anything you've ever witnessed. The light is so intense it nearly blinds you. All electric appliances around you begin to emit a disturbing, buzzing drone.

Iris's dog runs towards the light, yapping. A blue beam emanates from the sphere, gently probing the dog, then the crowd. 

The beam picks Claire up off the ground, bathing her with diffuse, cold light. She ascends slowly towards the glowing sphere, bearing an ecstatic smile. As she gets closer to the Traveller and disappears in its intense light, her voice rips the night with horrified screams.

Who else does the Traveller rapture?

Roll 1d6

1. PC with lowest Weird  
2. Iris  
3. Iris’s dog  
4. Victoria  
5. PC with highest Weird   
6. PC with highest Weird

If a PC is raptured, the Traveller will drop them off in the desert after implanting the Nucleus.

After the rapture, the Traveller emits a blinding flash of light and vanishes at incredible speed. Darkness and cold descend. Chaos ensues.

Soon after, another light appears in the sky – it’s distant at first, but approaching fast, accompanied by a sound like thunder. It’s a Channel 7 chopper, carrying **Brooke**, a rising TV host. 

The crowd disperses. Some run, others start their vehicles. Alfred screams Claire's name. She’s gone.

### BEAT 2: The Astro-Archeologist

Sleep Country, Little Rock Library, Night

Two days later, or more.

Anyone who has been touched by the Traveller’s beam has been plagued by dreams. They are a Called (p.xx).  
At least one of the PCs is a Called, even if they did not touch the Traveller’s beam. This PC is also plagued by dreams.

**Dreams**  
The dreams of the Called are affected by the Traveller.

Roll 1d6:

1. **Four Wings Observatory.** A black-and-white, silent film. Scientists study a star map, arguing without sound.  
2. **Mass Grave.** An enormous pit dug into the streets of the Desert. You’re in it.  
3. **Channel 7 Talk Show.** A dog-sitter, a monk, and a soccer player discuss your mental health. Sounds like you’re having hallucinations.  
4. **Herald of Light.** A pale figure with no face appears in your bedroom (the Faceless God). Its head grows to the point that it swallows you and everything around you.  
5. **Waking Nightmare.** You wake in bed, stomach twisting in pain. Something grows under your skin, ready to burst. Are you even sleeping?  
6. **Desert Crossroads.** Wind howls through the empty landscape.

#### 

The days after the encounter with the Traveller felt empty, as if the whole Bay had crawled back into bed and locked the door. Through news and back channels, you hear of more encounters, yet Channel 7 dismisses them as mass hysteria, a “brain sickness”. Talk show witnesses, in tears, claim their memories are fabrications

Tonight’s bleak atmosphere hardly surprises you.

You find yourselves in Little Rock, a dull residential area in the Desert district. It's 8pm. The streets are dead silent – not even a stray cat can be heard. An unsettling quiet hangs over the neighborhood. Most houses have their shutters closed, as if holding their breath.

A feeling of general distrust slowly consumed the Desert district. Those who dream of the Traveller, the **Called**, have learned to be discreet, exchanging flyers and pamphlets at bus stops and organizing secret support groups. The party is heading to one tonight at the Little Rock Library. The meeting is organized by **Luc**, the chief librarian and secret member of the **Collectors** (p. XX). 

**Questions: You’ll meet at the library with one or more survivors of the first encounter. Which ones? And how did you get in touch with them?**

#### The Library

The library has three main areas:

**Reading room**, containing books, shelves, chairs, and a fire extinguisher.  
**Archeology museum**  
**Two small offices and restrooms**.

#### Reading Room

Uncomfortable, plastic folding chairs line the space, facing a large TV screen. Luc is struggling to connect the A/V cables of the VHS deck to the screen. Most chairs remain empty, and those now arriving are late, their faces marked with worry. **Angela** helps them to the chairs. You recognize a couple of folks from the landing site in the desert.**.**

At the welcome table, **Sofia** offers cheap cookies and hot coffee in plastic cups. One wall is plastered with red “Have you seen him/her?” flyers. On the opposite side, framed photos of ancient, painted rocks depict a variety of scenes: a giant spider in the sky, floating figures, a mass grave. The drawings on the rocks are time-worn and hard to make out.  
One large framed photo stands out: a massive, painted boulder in the desert. Luc and a teenage girl pose at its base. They look happy.

**Luc**, an unusually tall and thin thirty-year-old man with a hairless face, starts the VHS player. A poorly produced documentary flickers to life, featuring brief interviews with the Called, interspersed with shaky footage of Luc, a librarian and self-taught archaeo-astronomer, guiding the camera through the archeology museum to examine painted rocks.

Standing awkwardly beside the TV, Luc provides live commentary for unfinished sequences. After the documentary, he opens the floor to the audience: “If you’re with us tonight, it means you’ve been guided by visions or dreams, or have encountered these beings. I’d love to hear your story and answer your questions.” 

A Called (p.xx) is the first to speak: "The beam touched me, it burned my face. My friends won’t talk to me anymore. My parents don’t even know I’m here tonight. I feel so ashamed... I hate feeling like this."

Other audience members speak up:

Roll 1d6:

1. *Hi, I’m **Brooke**, reporter at Channel 7\. We have a camera outside and would like to interview anyone who has met the aliens.*  
2. *I saw their master. It has no face, aliens bow to it. I was hiding under a bus in the Desert. Now I can’t sleep anymore, they haunt my dreams.*  
3. *(Standing up) This can’t be true, it’s bullshit, you’re all sick in the head.*  
4. *Their world is magnificent and made of platinum. I made drawings of it, look\!*  
5. *They talk to me in song. I’ve already heard their song before, but I can’t remember where.*  
6. *I feel so lonely. I’m happy to be here tonight.*

Question: Luc invites you to talk. What do you share?

##### Luc’s Plan

Luc is on edge, so close to his goal. He *needs* a Nucleus. His obsessive study of the painted rocks and archives has convinced him that hosting a Nucleus will grant him power over life and death. And he intends to use it to bring his daughter, Evangeline, back from the dead. He's completely delusional.

Luc has faith that someone in the audience is carrying a Nucleus. He just has to find them, someone who has gotten too close to the Traveller, or has been lifted by the beam.

Once Luc identifies a candidate (either a PC or NPC), he patiently lures them into the Archeology Museum likely under false pretenses. He might invite them to examine the rocks, maps, or documents, or even suggest a medical exam which, of course, doesn’t exist.

Once the potential Nucleus carrier is locked in the room, Angela helps Luc sedate them and carry them to her van. Luc plans to transport the carrier to Four Wings Observatory to perform the Nucleus extraction. Angela pretends to not know the details, and will go home once Luc drives away. She helps reluctantly, but, no matter what happens, she won’t turn against Luc.

Luc and Angela are not experienced criminals. Whatever they attempt will be messy and noisy.

#### Archeology Museum

A storage space and museum hybrid, boxes filled with archives, general purpose tools, and broken computers Several rocks bearing ancient paintings stand out on metallic shelves..

Luc’s desk stands in a corner of the museum. It’s covered in photos, maps, and research documents, including newspaper clippings from scammy UFO magazines.

Additionally, the desk drawers contain:

* Audio recordings of various witnesses of the Traveller  
* A human anatomy book covered in notes and doodles of a Nucleus being extracted from a belly  
* A True Church leaflet with a drawing of the Faceless God and a hand written question mark on the face  
* The diary of a 12-year-old girl detailing her illness (Luc’s daughter)  
* Family snapshots with Luc’s face systematically carved out of all photos

On closer inspection the painted rocks reveal recurrent motifs: mass graves, a crowd praying to the sun, a floating, spider-like being (the Traveller), and a long, elongated figure with no face, towering over all the others (the Faceless God). This is not the first time the Traveller has visited the Bay, as these paintings show.

### BEAT 3: The Nucleus {#beat-3:-the-nucleus}

Desert, Back road, Night

One week later.

#### Scene

Since the library meeting, you've holed up at home for a few days, your nights tormented by restless dreams. Your numbness is interrupted by the phone ringing: **Alfred** is on the line. He’s heard about a location deep in the Desert, near the abandoned railway line. It’s there that raptured citizens resurface. He needs to go there: it might be the only chance to find Claire. He doesn’t have the courage to do it alone and begs you to join him.

Tonight’s plan is to head to the rumored resurfacing point near the old railway line and, hopefully, find Claire.

You fell asleep during the trip and just woke up. It’s the dead of night. You’re inside Alfred's camper van, parked at a small gas station, the last beacon of electric light before the dusty darkness of the Desert. The engine is idling, yet the driver's seat is empty. 

Questions: 

* If you’ve already had the dreams of the Called, they become recurrent. Describe how they get worse and affect you.  
* Why did you agree to help Alfred? Is it out of pure generosity, or is there something else at stake for you?

Alfred is filling up the truck, his face etched with hope and anxiety. He’s lost considerable weight and looks under terrible pressure. The gas station clerk, a teen named **Jon**, is a total jerk but he has knowledge of the whereabouts of the old railroad. You’ll need to leave the main roads and cruise over dirt and rocks to reach it. It’s secluded, surrounded only by the night and silence. 

If Alfred is left alone with Jon, the two will argue about the current events and won't stop until one slaps the other.

**On the truck’s airwaves**  
Roll 1d6:

1. A desperate caller looking for their missing partner, who was taken by the light.  
2. A cryptic message in binary.  
3. New Age music: initially soothing, but becomes repetitive and annoying.  
4. A Channel 7 talk show: a psychologist explains mass hysteria, collective hallucinations and how to protect oneself.  
5. Traffic information: avoid roads tonight as several accidents have been reported (false).  
6. News flash: 20th anniversary of the Four Wings Observatory incident (no details given).

To reach the old railroad, the players must succeed on an Action Roll. If they fail, they end up in a random place.

**Where do you end up?**  
Roll 1d6:

1. Back at the gas station. Lights off, deserted. The clerk is dead, a lump of bloody flesh.  
2. Stranded in the desert. Clothes are scattered everywhere, belonging to all sorts of people.  
3. The original landing site. A burned-out truck. Trash. A sign made of aluminum foil reads: "Please come back\!"  
4. Channel 7 mobile production truck. No gas left. No one here.  
5. A few-days-dead corpse, its belly ripped open and crawling with bugs. Desert sand partially covers its face.  
6. An abandoned railway line. Straight and seemingly endless.

Once the vehicle reaches its destination (any destination), a pale, humanoid figure appears out of the dark. It's **Claire**: she looks well except her skin appears bleached, and she’s wearing nothing but a dirty T-shirt. 

Alfred sobs like a child as he holds her in his arms. He covers her cheeks and forehead with gentle kisses before taking her to the camper van.

He has so many questions and, as Claire answers, his mood shifts slowly from relieved to worried. Claire’s belly shows a small bump. Touching it reveals a badly sutured wound, emanating a slight, unearthly cold. The wound is slightly bleeding and thin, iridescent tentacles wriggle their way out. 

Claire speaks. “It put the light inside me. I can feel it. It’s telling me you are in pain. It can help you, just open yourself to it”

**After fifteen minutes**, the belly scar widens, revealing a luminous lump of iridescent filaments: a **Nucleus**. Claire’s face lights up with a euphoric grin.

**If you don’t find a way to contain the Nucleus**, it grows far too large for the opening in Claire’s belly. It forces its way out, hungry for a new host, and it’ll target one of the players (see Nucleus p.xx). Claire’s body splits in half as an amalgam of light and writhing tendrils expands outward, reaching for whoever is near. Claire dies in the process. Everyone rolls a Weird Save. If Alfred is driving when the Nucleus opens, he is likely to crash.

If it does not find a host, the Nucleus remains anchored to the corpse, puppeteering Claire’s lifeless body with awkward, jerking motions.

#### Moving On

At the end of this Beat, the players likely have either found ways to defeat the Nucleus, or one of PCs has become the new carrier.

* If the players are actively looking for more clues, or are tracked by Luc, proceed to [BEAT 4: Four Wings Observatory](#beat-4:-four-wings-observatory).  
* If the players don’t have leads to follow after the encounter with the Nucleus, proceed to [BEAT 5: The Final Encounter](#beat-5:-the-final-encounter).

### BEAT 4: Four Wings Observatory  {#beat-4:-four-wings-observatory}

Desert, Four Wings Observatory, Dawn

Four hours later

#### Scene

One or more players might have fallen into Luc’s custody. The others are outside the Observatory. They arrive in Alfred’s van or by foot as the Collectors exit the building with Alex.

Dawn breaks slowly over the eastern horizon, the light creeping through the shattered remnants of the observatory. You wake up to aches in every part of your body. You sit on the cold, hard ground, surrounded by dust and shards of broken glass, the weight of electric cables biting into your skin as they restrain you. With you are **Cesar** and his teenage son, **Isma**. Both are tied up, sunburned, dehydrated, and terrified. **Cesar carries a Nucleus.**

**Alex**, Cesar's partner, is just outside the observatory door, their voice trembling with fear. They’re supplicating to Luc and two other Collectors to let their family go.

Questions: You’ve been here before, as a teenager, looking for someone or something. What was it?

#### Four Wings Observatory

The observatory is a century-old relic, its dome half-crumbled. A giant telescope lies in the center of the room, broken and abandoned. The building feels like the hollow shell of a long-dead creature, its walls teetering on the edge of collapse. Swarms of small, iridescent birds have made it their home.

Found within the observatory:

* **Old star maps and computer printouts** that indicate an anomaly in space. The computers are cubic machines the size of a fridge, covered in lifeless LEDs and screens. A bright light approaching Earth has been tracked. It appeared out of nowhere in a remote region of space.   
* **A 1” magnetic tape audio track** with the voice of the Traveller. The recordings are filled with static noise, but they speak your name **(Weird save)**.  
* **A corpse covered by a curtain**. It’s someone from the first night. Their belly is ripped open. Next to them, traces of blood lead to a crack in the outside wall. Their Nucleus birthed and disappeared. The Collectors are aware of this fact and extremely disappointed.

If the computers are powered back on, the printers spit a series of letters and numbers. When deciphered, they reveal a message sent by the Traveller to the Faceless God: “Master, you’ll like humans. Their skin is so soft, their heart so dark.”

#### Outside the Observatory

Two Collectors, **Joan** and **Amel**, are debating with **Luc** about what to do with the potential Nucleus carriers. Joan is Amel’s boss. Joan’s car, an old, bulky sedan, is parked in the dust.

Joan, Amel, and Luc briefly listen to Alex’s plea. Then, Amel takes them inside the observatory with the other prisoners. 

Inside the observatory, the situation is at a standstill until the Nucleus starts bursting through Cezar’s belly.

At the end of this Beat, the players likely have either found ways to defeat the Nucleus, or one of them has become the new carrier.

* Whatever happens, whether the players manage to negotiate their way out or the situation is resolved in blood, move to [BEAT 5: The Final Encounter.](#beat-5:-the-final-encounter)


### BEAT 5: The Final Encounter {#beat-5:-the-final-encounter}

Deeper in the desert, Day

**All threads converge here**. This is the final Beat, the ultimate confrontation with the Traveller. At this point, the players are most likely accompanied by other survivors, travelling together in a vehicle or walking desperately through the desert. There is nothing except the sun, sand, hot wind, and them.

The Traveller appears high in the sky, a blinding sphere of light. Electric appliances and vehicles shut down as the air thickens with an ominous hum and the wind begins to rise. The ground beneath the characters vibrates with an unsettling energy as the Traveller descends toward them.

**Question**: As you see the light heralding the arrival of the Traveller, you picture with clarity the chain of events that brought you here. What could you have done differently?

Nucleus carriers feel an irresistible pull toward the light. Their Nuclei are drawn to reunite with their creator, **and they will unless something opposes them.**

* Any Nucleus carried by PCs (and NPCs) will try to birth to rejoin with the Traveller.  
* If no PCs carry a Nucleus, the Traveller will attempt to rapture the PC with the highest Weird. If the Traveller is successful, it speaks in the PC’s head with a meaningless, yet understandable song of white noise and drops something into their mouth: a tiny Nucleus which will birth years from now when the Traveller returns.

The rapture of a PC may be avoided if the characters act quickly by hiding, creating a clever distraction, or finding shelter. They might even attempt to strike a bargain, offering anything of value to the Traveller.

**What else happens?**

Roll 1d6

1. A small Channel 7 van arrives. **Brooke** steps out, gripping a small VHS camera. Roll 1d6 even: she gets too close to the Traveller. odd: she carries a Nucleus.  
2. A sand storm rages. It hurts.  
3. A **Nucleus** crawls out of the sand and attacks a PC.  
4. The Traveller howls a loud baseline for a handful of seconds. Get temporary hearing loss unless you find a way to protect yourself.  
5. The light emanating from the Traveller burns skin like fire.   
6. The air is loaded with Weird, succeed at a Weird save or get a Scar.

#### Closing the Session

After the final encounter, the Traveller will most likely vanish as abruptly as it appeared, leaving behind a suffocating silence. If you’re lucky enough you might have been left unscathed by the encounter. 

Over the next few days, the Lost Bay slowly returns to its usual weirdness. Bayers are always fast to forget and move on to the next thing. Support group pamphlets disappear and Channel 7 shifts its focus. Those few days feel like a temporary fever, an illusion.

You stop having nightmares.

**Recap what happened to the major NPCs:**

* How were they affected by their encounters with the Traveller?  
* Which NPCs survived unscathed?  
* Are they still in touch with the player characters?

**Describe how the aftermath affect factions and locations:**

* Brooke Shields, if she’s alive, becomes the new Channel 7 star anchorwoman.  
* The Collectors retreat into anonymity.  
* Little Rock Library is closed and emptied of its contents.

**Ask the players about their characters:**

* Are they still carrying a Nucleus?  
* Did they strike a deal with the Traveller?  
* How have their lives been impacted by the encounter?  
* How are the Conditions gained from the encounter affecting them?  
* What will they do in the coming days?

### NPCs

#### The Traveller

Miracle

A herald of the Faceless God. A sentient probe. As luminescent as it is cold, with a sole purpose of understanding humans and unveiling their vulnerabilities. It takes the shape of an iridescent cluster of filaments wrapped by a sphere of intense light. It can infect living beings with Nuclei, of which it shares a common consciousness. The Traveller is merciless in the execution of its duty.

**Wants:** To infect humans with Nuclei and learn their vulnerabilities.

**Mood:** Inquisitive. “Teach me what it is that you fear most, and I’ll spare your weak, soft body.”

**Weakness:** Terrified of the Faceless God and will believe anyone who claims to be in touch with it.

**Moves:**

* **Rapture** a creature in a beam of light  
* **Appear** as a tall, faceless human and mesmerize  
* **Inject** a Nucleus into a victim’s belly  
* **Fly** at high speed  
* **Animate** a dead mammal (when in Nucleus form)

**Quirk:** Accompanied by dusty winds and deep, drone-like sounds. The skies darken, leaving the Traveller as the sole light source. White, fibrous filaments sprout where it appears**.**

**Gear:** None

##### Nucleus

A detached part of the Traveller’s body and consciousness, a lump of iridescent tentacles and light. It has limited movement capability, but can animate a freshly dead mammal.

##### Infection

After a Nucleus is implanted, the infection will spread, causing a new symptom with each new Beat or whenever the character’s Weird increases:

* Illnesses and  injuries suddenly heal.  
* Insomnia arrives at night, and terrifying hallucinations during the day.  
* Small, iridescent tentacles grow like hair.  
* A growth forms in the belly, giving the shape of pregnancy.  
* The Nucleus hatches through the flesh and takes control of the carrier after they die.

A sound recordist with red hair and porcelain skin. Former Channel 7 employee who left for ethical reasons. Self-taught parent, constantly trying to do better and protect their family.

**Wants:** To save Cezar and Isma.

**Mood:** Pleading. “Cesar and Isma’s sunburns have nothing to do with the Traveller. They don’t carry a seed. Please, let them go\!”

**Quirk:** Constantly on the edge of crying. Never does.

#### Alfred

Former literature teacher who’s been enjoying every day of his life as a retiree, because it brings him closer to his love, Claire. Until she fell sick. He pretends to be convinced she’s going to be ok, but is constantly on the verge of breaking down.Knows everything about schools and libraries. Cherishes his camper.

**Mood:** Hopeful. “Look at the sky, the sand\! There's nowhere quite like this. Even if they don't come tonight, I'm happy to have spent the night here and met such nice folks.”

**Wants:** To forget, just for a while, the hardships he’s going through.

**Gear:** Multi-tool knife. Pendant with Claire’s photo.

**Quirk:** Wears a grey ponytail and bears an uncanny resemblance to a famous actor, though you can't quite place who.

**Moves:**
